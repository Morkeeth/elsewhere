# Elsewhere decision record

This record distinguishes the product owner's decisions from Codex-assisted implementation work for OpenAI Build Week.

## Human product decisions

- **User and wedge:** launch with ambitious young professionals making career or location decisions. Other journeys demonstrate the engine's range but are not the judged claim.
- **Product promise:** Elsewhere rehearses competing futures; it does not tell someone which life to choose.
- **Trust boundary:** ledger metrics are deterministic and traceable. GPT-5.6 may interpret those metrics but cannot become their source.
- **Disagreement:** four independent value lenses are retained rather than collapsed into a recommendation.
- **Safety:** shocks have explicit modeled effects; the product reports scenario risk, not a prediction of a person's future.
- **Scope removed:** authentication, billing, rosters, social profiling, and OpenClaw delivery are outside the hackathon demo.

## Codex-assisted work

Codex accelerated the Next.js application, typed Zod data contracts, deterministic payroll/currency/budget calculations, GPT-5.6 Responses integration, responsive decision journey, evidence surface, regression tests, release checks, and this decision record. The product owner reviewed and approved the problem framing, value lenses, safety constraints, and public release.

## GPT-5.6 runtime role

Four independent GPT-5.6 Sol Responses calls receive the same immutable baseline and shocked future record with distinct protected values: financial resilience, belonging and relationships, reversibility and optionality, and adversarial failure and regret. The disagreement matrix is model-generated; deterministic code owns every experiment duration, cost, action template, and outcome number. A fifth constrained response selects only the qualitative uncertainty category the deterministic experiment should test.
