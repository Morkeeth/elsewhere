# Submission copy

## One line

Elsewhere lets four AI witnesses inhabit grounded versions of your future, breaks each life with the same shock, and gives you one reversible experiment instead of a verdict.

## Short description

Consequential decisions are not single questions. They are competing futures with different failure modes. Elsewhere turns two to four life paths into inspectable twelve-month world states grounded in tax rules, rent, income, costs, and explicit personal assumptions. Four GPT-5.6 witnesses examine those immutable ledgers concurrently through financial, belonging, reversibility, and adversarial lenses. A fifth response synthesizes the disagreement without being allowed to change any number.

Then the user introduces a real shock—caregiving, job loss, health, visa, or any custom event—and every future reruns from the selected month. The product reveals where the lives diverge, when each door becomes expensive to reopen, and the smallest fourteen-day experiment that can replace imagination with evidence.

## What makes it technically distinctive

- Four concurrent GPT-5.6 witnesses produce genuinely independent interpretations, followed by a fifth structured synthesis.
- A deterministic simulation engine owns every numeric output; AI only interprets the ledger.
- A provenance audit traces every numeric summary to formulas and dated sources.
- The shock engine recomputes the full causal timeline rather than adding narrative commentary.
- OpenClaw delivery moves the experiment into the user’s action channel.

## Judge test

1. Open **Decision +** and change salary, rent, or shock timing.
2. Run the futures and verify that the ledger appears immediately while witnesses work.
3. Toggle the shock and inspect the divergence and commitment markers.
4. Open **Evidence** and follow one official source.
5. Export the Markdown brief and JSON world states.
6. Check `/api/health` for configured witness and delivery readiness.

## Built with

Next.js, TypeScript, React, OpenAI Responses API on GPT-5.6, Zod structured validation, SVG, OpenClaw-compatible webhook delivery, and Vercel.
