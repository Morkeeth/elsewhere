# Elsewhere Vision — The Personal Decision Twin

## The premise

Some people keep journals, decision logs, complaint books, notes, lists, screenshots, and half-finished arguments because they are already doing research on their own lives.

Elsewhere turns that private research practice into a rehearsal engine.

It does not predict a life, impersonate a loved one, or decide for someone. It helps a person make their assumptions visible, rehearse plausible pressure, identify the perspectives they are carrying, and run the smallest real experiment that improves the next decision.

> Don’t ask AI what to choose. Build a better model of what changes when you choose.

## The category

**A personal decision twin**: a private, evidence-linked model of how a person makes consequential decisions under uncertainty.

Unlike a future-self chatbot, Elsewhere has a memory of:

- the futures a person considered;
- the assumptions that made each future attractive;
- the concerns they attributed to people who matter;
- the shocks they rehearsed;
- the experiments they ran; and
- where their prior model of life was right, wrong, or incomplete.

## Product principles

1. **Rehearsal, not prediction.** Outputs describe scenario risk and unknowns, never a forecast of another person or a guaranteed future.
2. **Evidence before eloquence.** Facts, user assumptions, and model interpretation remain visibly separate.
3. **Perspectives are authored, not impersonated.** “My model of Mum’s protective concern” is valid. “What Mum will think” is not.
4. **Every strong claim is editable.** A user can challenge an assumption, revise a context lens, or mark evidence stale.
5. **The exit is real life.** The system earns trust only by producing reversible experiments and learning from what happened.
6. **Private by default.** No silent inbox, journal, contact, or cloud scraping. Every artifact is deliberately selected and attributed.

## The enduring loop

```text
Capture → Model → Rehearse → Test → Remember → Calibrate
```

The current hackathon app covers Model → Rehearse → Test. The long-term product adds Capture, Remember, and Calibrate.

## The decisive insight

The product should not optimize for “confidence.” It should optimize for **better questions before commitment**:

- What am I assuming?
- Whose concern am I carrying?
- Which condition would change the answer?
- What is the cheapest evidence I can collect this week?
- What did I get wrong last time?

## Positioning

**Your notes app, but with consequences.**

For the decision you keep reopening at 1:14am.

## Explicit non-goals

- Relationship or family-member prediction
- Contact impersonation or simulated DMs presented as real people
- Therapy, diagnosis, legal, financial, or immigration advice
- Automated action on behalf of a user
- A generic life-coach chat interface
